Community 10,000: Helping Oxfam

Home

TI had been aware of the good things Community 10,000 do through colleagues being involved previously with various projects around Leeds, but I’d never had the opportunity to participate myself. So when Bryan Sharp, Delivery Manager for the new style ESA service, organised some volunteering days I jumped at the chance to get involved.

So, on Tuesday 18 th December 7 colleagues and myself headed to the Oxfam national textile recycling centre in Batley to provide our services for the day.

On arrival we met Holly, our hostess for the day, who told us some history and information about the Oxfam charity. She gave us a tour around the vast warehouse units where clothes and textiles are delivered on a daily basis, graded, sorted and shipped out across the world. Our tasks for the day would be listing items for the Oxfam online store, picking and packing orders, preparing the clothing and taking photos to list the items online.

We were kept busy all day and as we were packing up to leave Holly thanked us for our time and calculated the amount we had raised for the Oxfam charity, over £1,000!! Everyone enjoyed the day and felt like out little of volunteering had added to the great work Oxfam do to eradicating poverty.
