Legacy is not the past nor the future, but it is the present

By Lindsey Broderick

Home

Whilst Digital Delivery teams progress projects with new technologies to drive the digital transformation of DWP, our customers and Operational colleagues continue to rely on our Legacy systems to claim and administer welfare benefits.

Here in Health we support key Legacy systems which are used by over 4,000 agents to process over 1 million claims to Health Benefits annually - so it’s vital that we remain focused on the systems which support DWPs current business.

One of the Health systems I’m responsible for is ESA CAM which is a Siebel application introduced into DWP back in 2013. Whilst relatively new in terms of Legacy, it still presents challenges to those who use it.

Last month we successfully implemented a major release to deliver key system improvements. As these were covered under the existing AM contract, there was no additional cost to either Digital or Operations – yet the benefits will save ££££s in terms of processing efficiencies.

Key Release Scope
Improvement Description Average Time Savings
Move Customer Contact Number to My Cases View Agents can view customer Mobile number without snapshotting the NINO 3 minutes per claim, Also supports Robotics initiatives for SMS Service
Allow agents to action multiple (rather than single line) Deletes in the ‘Other Benefits’ View Agents can now select multiple entries for deletion, rather than repeating a single action multiple times 1 minute per claim
Deliver the Design Solution for the Top reported application errors Introduction of a new button to merge information in CAM and CIS to eradicate historical data errors 7 minutes per impacted case. Scans identified that errors 2 & 3 combined occurred almost 2,500 each week.

The future of the application is under review at the moment - with the rollout of Universal Credit playing the largest influence…but rather than stop work, we’re still very much driven by the fact we support thousands of agents on a system which still processes over 25,000 claims per month – so whilst there is still opportunity to improve the user experience, generate efficiencies and strengthen the service we provide our customers, we remain passionate about the present, and the future of course!......
