Access to Work: "I support help for work... (..because of a disability or health condition)

By Mike Renyard

Home

We’ve been working hard over the last few weeks to stand up the “Report your patient is terminally ill” service (digital DS1500) in AWS. This has been particularly difficult because the service is actually accessed direct from the NHS Health and Social Care network (HSCN – what was N3). It’s involved a lot of tricky configuration to prove the authentication will function correctly in AWS – including a direct VPN link to HSCN, setting up actual NHS smartcards and the software to run them and working with our NHS colleagues to steer this through.

At the same time we’ve been onboarding to a connection to NHSCN via the SDX service and this has also been tricky – largely due the way in which SDX dealt with the connection, meaning we had an incompatible approach that SDX were (at the time of onboarding, unable to work around. Cue much work on our side to build a workaround in AWS to align to SDX. Meanwhile our Chris Brundell and SDX’s Jo Knight collaborated brilliantly to test approaches and connections , finally solving the tricky DNS resolution on SDX side at the eleventh hour!

Following some stringent testing of the solution (a whole afternoon spent trying to break it!!) we were ready last week to deploy and by Friday, after NHS colleagues had made the DNS changes at their end, we saw the first DS1500 forms pushing through AWS directly into DRS. A slightly hairy ride at tmes but thanks to the great work of Chris Brundell, Chris Hulley and Jo Knight (and a few friends in the NHS) we’re now able to draw a line under UK Cloud, saving costs of over £600 per day.
