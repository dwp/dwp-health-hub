The Digital Delivery team on Access to Work have had a few changes in the team make-up recently. We’ve taken this opportunity to review what determines success for our intended new digital service. We’ve been working and checking in with a range of senior stakeholders as well as reviewing our initial Discovery findings on key user needs. We’ve created an outline roadmap that now clearly sets out the rollout of the new digital service and how this also seeks to ‘retire’ the current legacy system.

Our immediate focus has additionally been to ensure that from the onset, we develop a collaborative project delivery environment with our colleagues in OED, Operations and Policy.

As the first objective set out in our roadmap, the team have just kick started the new service development for handling renewals of existing grants.

The past week has been spent bringing the whole ‘extended’ team together to undertake spot checks reviewing our initial assumptions and previously determined user needs. We are now at the stage where we have our first draft of the initial Story map setting out the journey for renewals. Our intention is to firm up the journey during the next three weeks with exercises involving user testing with low fidelity prototypes and continued user research.
