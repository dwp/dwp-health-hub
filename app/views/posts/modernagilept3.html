Accelerate Modern Agility: Part Three

Home

Part three of a four part look into modern agility by Justin Coyne.

In Part 1 we identified our Values…. the things we hold dear to us, in Part 2 we discussed how those values are embodied in our leaders (remembering we are all leaders!) In part 3 we explore Management Practices….

Management Practices

We will remove our fascination with ‘A’gile ceremonies and place the focus on Results over Rituals. We will ask ourselves ‘Why are we conducting a stand up, retro or a review?’ Are we using these techniques with the intention behind them are just doing them with cult like behaviour.

Visible Flow with Visible Progress and Visible Quality

We will map out our value stream and track progress of work through this, by identifying the bottlenecks and providing interventions and changes where necessary we will improve the speed of the system to get the fundamentals embedded. Quality will be built in through agreed Definitions of Done and the shifting left of Testing and inclusion of Test Automation artefacts in the build pipeline.

Small batches of Customer Value

We will ensure all work undertaken is to deliver ‘something’ that the customer needs, and we will ensure that we keep the items delivered small, limiting the amount of Work In Progress at any one time in order to maximise throughput and so that we can deliver frequently.

Frequent customer feedback on experiments

We will ensure we promote experimentation through prototyping, research, lab testing, interviews, technical spikes, A/B testing, scenarios and feature toggles to name a few examples to gather customer feedback on the work we are completing to inform our decisions and priorities.

Monitoring and Proactive Notifications

We will provide highly visible and evidence based feedback on the work that we do, and implement timely monitoring and notifications such that we are the people to first find out if we have an issue. This feedback loop will help to influence the priority of future work

Technical Practices

We are fortunate enough to have some experienced hands, technical wizardry and inquisitive minds on the team who can implement some industry best practice for us to make this endeavour possible. We will place an increased focus in the following areas to build quality in as standard and maintain a sustainable flow through the system.

Clean Code, SOLID Principles and Design Patterns

We will use industry best practise and learn from previous community learning, by making our code understandable flexible and maintainable, we will also facilitate component re-use and build in extensibility. Code is just written once and and then forgotten about, it is reviewed, extended, moved multiple times over the life of the project. If we don’t write good code, then the code, the tests, the extensibility will all suffer. If we don’t have the confidence in changing it then it will rot.

Emergent Architecture

The design emerges because there is no up-front design phase, we design for what we know now, leaving decisions until the last responsible moment, and even then building in as much flexibility for future change as possible. Intentional architecture is not ignored, if we really do have the answer now, then use it…. And for some things (eg. Programming language) you have to decide early and with intent.

Empowered teams

We will include the right Technical and Assurance skills in the team for them to be autonomous and make decisions. We will involve Security and architecture and allow a lightweight Change Management process for Live deployments avoiding ‘risk management theatre’.

Pairing/ Mobbing

We recognise that there is benefit in collaborative working and we will not judge the development team by the ‘amount’ of code generated at all. We will commit to exploring the use of Pairing and Mobbing as the normal for the teams but experimenting to find the balance for our specific situation. Collaboration promotes: safety (will try new things out more in pairs or as a mob), knowledge sharing and collective code ownership (you learn the problem and solution together), quality (pairing / mobbing have often demonstrated better quality), performance (it’s not the number of lines of code written or the speed of typing that takes the time – it is the thinking that does it. Working in pairs / mobs has also shown evidence that things get done quicker).

Test Driven

We will involve the entire development team in the building and maintenance of the tests, to force the creation of testable designs, clean code and whole team involvement in the test automation suites. Thus closing the feedback loop in that tests aren’t done at the end while the developers are now working on something else. We will make current environments builds available for QA to complete manual, exploratory, and usability testing.

Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery

We will adopt a set of capabilities that enable us to deliver value to our users, quickly, safely and sustainably. We will Enable early identification of issues, work in small chunks, automate repetitive work, keep improving and maintain overall focus on organisational goals. It is worthy of a specific mention that we will strive to perform Trunk Based development and have as few as possible feature branches. We will avoid ‘Code Freezes’ and stabilisation periods.

Version Control

A critical component of Continuous Integration and Delivery is the comprehensive use of version control. We will maintain application code, system configuration, application configuration build automation and configuration to allow us improve. We will be confident that we can build, deploy and roll back (if required) quickly and with success.

…. Now we are talking, this is stuff that we can really get hold of and start doing, there is some good stuff in here, want more….
