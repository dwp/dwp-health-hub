How do we establish if we are improving?....Measure it ….. But what to measure?

We believe that you improve at the things that you measure. Within ‘Accelerate’ a clear set of performance metrics are identified, with which a correlation can be seen between low, medium and high performing organisations. To counteract the tendency to ‘game’ the metrics these have been selected to complement each other such that we do not to prioritise team performance over service quality.

Lead time to Value

This describes the time it takes to go from a customer making a request for something of value to them to the request being satisfied. In terms of our KANBAN, it will be the time from a Story Card being Started (in Dev) to being ‘Released to Live’.

Release Frequency

How frequently are we delivering value to our live environment? It is only when a release happens that the user’s has access to what has been completed. By choosing to monitor and by seeking an upward trend in Release frequency it will help to keep batch sizes small. It will ensure that we have a simple practiced deployment process to reduce risk and overhead and accelerate feedback.

Mean Time To Recover (MTTR)

Given that failures are unavoidable, how long does it to recover? We can positively effect this metric by the releasing at a high frequency, and by keeping releases small we will make it easy to unpick any defects or failures. Through Proactive notification and monitoring we can detect problems before they happen, isolate them…..fix them. We can do this much quicker and reduce the MTTR.

Frequent customer feedback on experiments

We will ensure we promote experimentation through prototyping, research, lab testing, interviews, technical spikes, A/B testing, scenarios and feature toggles to name a few examples to gather customer feedback on the work we are completing to inform our decisions and priorities.

% Change Failure

How often are we implementing a change that fails? We recognise that something’s will slip through our net. We will track the software releases and infrastructure changes that degrade our main service or require remediation work as a key metric. We will positively affect this by building quality in as standard, by automating as much as possible and by keeping releases small and frequent.

Progress towards Service Outcomes

What is the difference that our product is making in live operations? We will identify the Key Objectives and Results that are important to the business (this will be our Roadmap too!) and monitor our performance against these. We will also use these metrics as guidance for prioritization.

Everyone loves a metric….We believe that these are simple, repeatable and provide an indication of performance, with sufficient insight such that we can take action to change future behavior. We also believe that these metrics will drive us towards the values defined earlier…
